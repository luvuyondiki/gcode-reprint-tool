How it works

      
    
        
  1. Upload the original G-code from your slicer.
    2. 
        
  2. Enter the Z height of the last good layer (not where the nozzle is now).
    3. 
        
  3. Enter the machine's current X, Y, Z from the LCD or send M114.
    4. 
        
  4. The tool trims everything below that layer and moves the nozzle with explicit G-code (no G92).
    5. 
        
  5. After generating, use the built-in path preview to verify the resume join — check for gaps or travel over printed areas.
    6. 
        
  6. Do not home the printer after a failure — send M114 first. Homing Z to the top will break coordinates.
    7. 
      

      
The preview shows printed paths, resume travel, and new extrusion. You can still load the file in your slicer viewer for a final check.
